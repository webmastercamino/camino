Stories from the field

Real people. Real homes.

Every home we build has a story — the family who waited years for safety, and the team that showed up to build it. These are some of those stories.

Families transformed

Home #83 · 2024
The Flores Family — Northern Honduras
José and Elena Flores had been on CAMINO's waiting list for three years. When their home was completed in March 2024, their daughter — who had been missing school regularly due to illness from their previous living conditions — enrolled in the local school for the first time. She finished the year with the highest marks in her class.
Northern Honduras · March 2024 build team
⛪
Community · 2023
A new church becomes a community anchor
When CAMINO planted a church in a rural community outside Rio Lindo in 2023, the pastor — a graduate of our trade school program — had no building. Within 18 months, the church had grown to 60 families. Today it runs after-school tutoring, a women's savings group, and emergency food distribution during the rainy season.
Rio Lindo region · CAMINO church planting program
🏠
Home #47 · 2022
The Rodríguez Family — Rio Lindo
For six years, María and her three children slept on a dirt floor under a tarp that leaked every rainy season. Her youngest had chronic respiratory infections. Two weeks after CAMINO completed their new home, the infections stopped. "My children sleep through the night now," she said at the dedication. "That is everything."
Rio Lindo, Cortes · January 2022 build team

What participants say

From first-time volunteers to families who come back year after year.

What struck me most was the dedication ceremony. The mother hadn't cried the whole week — she worked harder than any of us. But when Walker handed her the key, she just held it and shook. That image doesn't leave you.

Michael R. · Austin, TX · January 2025

Our church group of 12 had no construction experience between us. Walker's team taught us everything. By the end of the week we'd laid block, framed a roof, and built something we'll talk about for the rest of our lives. Book your trip now — you'll regret waiting.

Pastor James T. · First Baptist, Nashville · June 2024

I've been on three mission trips with other organizations. CAMINO is different. You're not just a tourist — you're actually building something real with your hands alongside the family who will live there. That key ceremony on the last day broke me in the best possible way.

Dave M. · Houston, TX · January 2024

I'm 67 and was worried I'd slow the group down. I couldn't lift block but I mixed cement for four straight days and never felt more useful in my life. There's a role for everyone. CAMINO staff made sure of it.

Barbara W. · Dallas, TX · October 2023

I was nervous bringing my 16-year-old daughter. By day three, she was teaching the Honduran kids English games during lunch breaks. She came home a different person. We've already signed up for next January.

I sponsor a home every year now. After I went on the trip and saw exactly where the money goes — met the family, watched the walls go up — there was never a question of stopping. $2,000 changes a family forever. That's nothing compared to what it gives.

Linda H. · Home sponsor since 2021
