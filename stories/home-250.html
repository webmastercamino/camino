How we got here

The road to 250

2014
CAMINO founded
Walker and Seiny Somerville move to Honduras as full-time missionaries. The vision for housing-first community development begins to take shape.
2017
Home #1 — the first family
Three years of groundwork culminate in the first CAMINO home. A foundation is poured, walls go up, and a family moves out of a wood-scrap shack for the first time.
2019
501(c)(3) incorporated · First Oklahoma team
Central American Ministry Outreach, Inc. officially incorporated in Texas. Church mission team partnerships begin to scale the build rate.
2020
Home #33 — Kelsey's Missions · Belen water well
Kelsey's Missions of Culpeper, VA sponsors a home. The Belen community water well is completed — ending a daily multi-hour water haul for 35 families. Hurricanes Eta & Iota strike Honduras; every CAMINO home stands.
2022–2024
Trade school graduates hired · Church plants take root
Trade school graduates join CAMINO's construction crews full-time. Two UMC church plants established. Juan Carlos — a scholarship recipient — becomes Walker's lead apprentice.
2026
Home #247 — Rivera family · Oklahoma team
The Oklahoma Mission Team and Stobtown Freewill Baptist Church of Lone Grove, OK sponsor Home #247 in the community of 20 de Julio. Julian's sister Reina — a prior home recipient — helps paint the new Welcome Wall. Read the full story →

Why 250 matters

250 families. A thousand lives.

The average Honduran family has 4 people. 250 homes means roughly 1,000 people — children, parents, grandparents — who no longer sleep under leaking tarps, on dirt floors, or in structures that wouldn't survive the next storm.

Walker's long-term vision extends beyond Honduras to all of Central America. Home #250 is not the finish line — it's proof of what's possible when a community of donors, churches, and volunteers commits to showing up year after year.
