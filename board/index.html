What-If Scenario Analysis
Specific high-impact scenarios with response plans. Board should review and assign owners annually.
🏥 Walker has a serious health crisis
Impact: All Honduras operations halt. Community partnerships at risk. 65+ waitlist families affected. Donor relationships severed. Estimated 6–12 month disruption.
Response plan (not yet written): Designate interim field director. Identify Honduran national co-leader. Cross-train on all donor relationships. Complete succession plan document (due Aug 15, 2026). Consider key-man life insurance.
👨‍👩‍👧 Walker family health crisis
Impact: Walker returns to US for extended period. Honduras programs pause. Staff team may fragment without Walker's relational leadership.
Response plan: Same as health crisis above. Additionally: establish CAMINO Honduras local leadership team so operations can continue in Walker's absence. Document all community partner contacts.
🗳️ Honduras geopolitical crisis (coup, election dispute)
Impact: US Embassy issues Level 4 "Do Not Travel." All scheduled trips cancelled. Walker and family may need emergency evacuation. Community projects suspended. Possible 1–2 year gap.
Response plan: Monitor State Dept. advisories monthly. Pre-define evacuation protocol with US Embassy (register at step.state.gov). Escrow trip deposits for 18 months. Maintain $15K emergency reserve for evacuation costs. Donors notified proactively via email.
🦠 Pandemic / global health emergency
Impact: International travel suspended. Mission trips cancelled for 1–2+ years. Trip fee revenue ($30K/yr) disappears. Construction halts. 2020 showed 55% drop in homes built.
Response plan: Convert to virtual fundraising immediately (virtual tours, live builds). Activate "pandemic fund" campaign targeting existing donors. Maintain Honduras local staff relationships remotely. Pre-approved 30% budget reduction scenario ready.
🌀 Category 4+ hurricane hits Cortés region
Impact: Rio Lindo retreat center damaged or destroyed. Community infrastructure lost. Homes CAMINO built may need reconstruction. Walker's team displaced. Build season delayed 3–6 months.
Response plan: Emergency campaign ("Honduras Hurricane Relief") — typically raises 2–3× normal. Redirect construction capacity to hurricane recovery. Contact UMCOR (United Methodist Committee on Relief) for disaster-response partnership. Insurance on the retreat center is critical.
⚖️ Volunteer injured on a build site
Impact: Medical costs $5,000–$50,000+. Without liability insurance, CAMINO is personally liable. Legal action possible. Volunteer program could be suspended. Mission trip revenue at risk.
Response plan: Requires general liability + volunteer accident insurance (currently unmitigated — CRITICAL). Require signed waivers from all participants. Ensure all volunteers have personal travel insurance as a trip requirement.
💸 Major donor (>20% of revenue) withdraws
Impact: $24,000+ annual gap. Honduras construction slows by 30%. Staff operations strained. May require emergency cuts to overhead.
Response plan: No single donor should exceed 15% of revenue (policy to be adopted). Diversify through corporate channel (Phase 2) and Mailchimp recurring donor program. Build operating reserve to 3-month minimum ($30K by 2027).
🇺🇸🇭🇳 US imposes Honduras travel ban or sanctions
Impact: All US volunteers barred from travel. Mission trip revenue ceases immediately. Walker (US citizen) may be unable to operate in-country. Organization mission threatened.
Response plan: Low probability but catastrophic. Mitigate by building Honduran national leadership team capable of independent operation. Maintain legal compliance with OFAC and State Dept. regulations. Consult international nonprofit attorney annually.
