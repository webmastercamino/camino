What to expect

A week that changes two worlds

Every morning, your team heads to an active construction site. You'll mix cement, lay block, carry materials, and work side by side with Honduran families and local CAMINO staff. No construction experience needed — just a willingness to show up and work hard.

Afternoons and evenings are for community. Meals with local families. Church services. Visits to schools CAMINO supports. Time to sit, listen, and understand the stories behind the work.

By the time you leave, you will have built something permanent — a home, a relationship, and a different way of seeing the world.

Ready to go?

Contact us to reserve spots for your team. We'll walk you through everything — costs, preparation, travel, and what to bring.

Common questions

What people ask before they go

Never been on a mission trip? That's fine — most of our volunteers haven't either.

🔨

Do I need construction experience?

Not at all. CAMINO staff leads every work site. You'll be taught exactly what to do. Willingness matters more than skill.

🌡️

Is Honduras safe?

CAMINO operates in established community partnerships with deep local trust. We take security seriously and have a strong safety record over years of hosting teams.

🗣️

Do I need to speak Spanish?

No. CAMINO provides translation throughout the trip. That said, learning even a few words goes a long way in building relationships.

👨‍👩‍👧

Can families with kids come?

Yes — we've had families with children as young as 10 on trips. We'll help you assess whether a specific trip is right for your family's ages and needs.

💰

How much does it cost?

Trip costs vary by duration and group size. Contact us for current pricing. Many participants fundraise to cover their trip costs — we can help with that too.

✈️

How do I get there?

Most teams fly into San Pedro Sula (SAP). CAMINO staff meets you at the airport and handles all in-country transportation from there.
