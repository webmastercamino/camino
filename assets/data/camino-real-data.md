**Honduras poverty statistics cited on site:**
- 73.6% of Honduran population in poverty (worsened from 60% in 2020 after Hurricanes Eta and Iota)
- 53.7% in extreme poverty
- ~18% in "super extreme poverty" (squatter communities)
- 69.8% live below the national poverty line (World Bank data, cited on /church page)
- Nearly 7 out of 10 Hondurans live on less than $2.50 per day

## PROGRAMS

### 1. Housing / Family Security
- Housing-first development model
- $2,000 builds a complete house (see full materials list below)
- 65 families currently on waiting list
- 100–115+ homes completed to date

**Full $2,000 house materials list (exact from /house-cost):**
- 21 = 1 in x 12 in x 12 ft boards
- 21 = 1 in x 12 in x 16 ft boards
- 5 = 4 in x 4 in x 10 ft posts
- 4 = 2 in x 4 in x 10 ft posts
- 3 = 2 in x 4 in x 16 ft rafters
- 14 = 2 in x 2 in x 10 ft roof supports
- 6 = 16 foot long metal roofing sheets
- 1 = Dump truck load of sand
- 10 = bags of portland cement
- 8 = Pounds of nails
- 85 = Roofing screws
- 1 = Gallon of termite treatment solution
- 1 = 5 Gallon bucket of paint (They pick the color)
- 1 = Paint kit of a roller, two brushes and paint pan
- 1 = Day's labor for a professional to pour the cement floor
- 1 = Set of door hardware (hinges, hasp, lock and key)

### 2. Evangelism and Discipleship (Ministry Outreach / Church Plant)
- Church plant: **El Camino** — at CAMINO retreat center, served by Walker Somerville (UMC-designated pastor)
- Second church plant: **El Belen community** — services held in CAMINO-built Christian activities center; permanent church structure planned for 2024
- Partner denomination: **United Methodist Church (UMC) of Honduras**
- Community church support: **Buena Vista Reformed Church** (small mountain church)
- Church project in **La Union**: community purchased land, needs $3,000 (wood) or $10,000 (cinderblock) building
- Leadership training classes for local church leaders underway

### 3. Education (SMA Scholarship Program)
- Program name: **SMA Scholarship Program**
- Details: Page exists (/sma-scholarship-program) but content not populated — no costs, student count, or school name published yet

### 4. Economic Development
- **Trade school** constructed and operational
- Trades training programs for poverty alleviation

### 5. Water and Solar Projects
- Solar power projects completed for: Diaz family, Martinez family, Paz family, Gomez family
- Water access projects planned/underway for Paz family
- Costs: vary by project (not published)

## MISSION TRIPS

**Trip structure:**
- Duration: 8 days
- Group size: Example given is 15 persons; min/max not stated
- Available: "A Few Available Dates" for summer and fall 2026

**Per-person costs:**
- Room & board: **$600** per person (covers food, lodging, transportation, side trip to nearby lake and waterfall)
- Project fund: **$500** per person (team collective)
- Combined example: "a 15 person team could raise the $7,500 (15 x $500) in project expenses" + $9,000 R&B = **$16,500 total** for a 15-person team

**Project types available:**
- House construction ($2,000/house)
- Church construction
- Community playgrounds
- Orphanage improvements
