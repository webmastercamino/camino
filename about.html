Our story

Born from a calling.
Built on faith.

CAMINO started with one family, one trip, and a conviction that safe housing changes everything. Twelve years later, we've built over 115 homes and watched entire communities transform.

How it started

Walker & Seiny Somerville

In 2014, Walker and Seiny Somerville moved to Honduras as full-time missionaries. What they saw — families living without safe shelter, children sleeping on dirt floors exposed to rain and parasites — moved them from missionaries to builders.

They founded CAMINO (Central American Ministry Outreach) on a simple premise: a safe home is the foundation for everything else. Health. Education. Faith. Family stability. Without it, nothing else takes root. The first home was built in fall 2017 — and CAMINO has been building ever since.

Since those first homes, CAMINO has grown into a community of volunteers, donors, and Honduran partners working together across 12+ communities in Cortés — one home at a time. 65 families are currently on the waitlist.

Mission & vision

Why we exist

Our mission is to demonstrate the love of Christ by partnering with families in Honduras to build safe homes and develop thriving communities.

Mission

To bring hope and transformation to families living in extreme poverty in Honduras through safe housing, community development, and the gospel of Jesus Christ.

Vision

Thriving Honduran communities where every family has a safe home, access to clean water and education, and is rooted in a community of faith.

Context

Understanding Honduras

Northern Honduras is one of the most economically vulnerable regions in Central America. Understanding this context shapes everything CAMINO does.

📊

Extreme poverty

Approximately 18% of Hondurans live in extreme poverty — surviving on less than $1.90 per day, without reliable access to shelter, food, or clean water.

🏚️

Housing crisis

Hundreds of thousands of Honduran families live in unsafe structures — wood scraps, tarps, and tin that offer little protection from weather, insects, or violence.

💧

Water access

In rural communities, women and children often walk miles daily to access water from contaminated rivers and streams, spending time that could be used for school or work.

🎒

Education barriers

Without a safe home, children miss school, struggle to study, and face higher risks of illness. Stable housing is the foundation that makes education possible.

💼

Economic cycle

Generational poverty is deeply entrenched. CAMINO's trade school and employment of local builders works to break that cycle with real economic opportunity.

✝️

Faith community

The church is a powerful community anchor in Honduras. CAMINO partners with local churches to provide not just housing, but lasting spiritual and social support.

How we work

Core values

These six values shape every decision CAMINO makes — from who we partner with to how we build.

✝️

Faith-driven

Everything we do flows from our faith in Jesus Christ. We serve because we are loved, and we build because we believe every person bears the image of God.

🤝

Community-rooted

We work alongside Honduran families and local leaders — not for them. Every project is a partnership, and every community shapes its own development.

🏗️

Housing-first

Safe shelter is the prerequisite for everything else. We build homes first because nothing else takes root without a stable, dry, secure place to call home.

📈

Sustainable impact

We build for the long term — investing in local employment, trade education, and community leadership so the impact outlasts any single trip or project.

🔍

Transparency

Every dollar is accounted for. We report openly on how funds are spent and the families they serve, because your trust is the foundation of our work.

🙌

Dignity

We come as learners, not rescuers. We honor the dignity, intelligence, and resilience of every family we serve, and we hold ourselves accountable to that.
