Our story

Faith in action.
Community transformed.

CAMINO began as one family's response to extreme poverty in Honduras. Today, it's a 501(c)(3) serving over 12 communities — and growing.

How it started

A family project that became a movement

In 2017, missionaries Walker and Seiny Somerville arrived in Honduras and found families living in conditions most people can't imagine — dirt floors, leaking roofs, no running water. They started small: build one home. Then another.

What began as a personal calling became Central American Ministry Outreach (CAMINO). In 2019, CAMINO became a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, enabling broader support and transparent accountability to donors.

"Building a new home is a new beginning for a family."

Today, CAMINO has built over 100 homes, dug wells, constructed bridges, launched a trade school, and planted churches — all across 12+ communities in Honduras.

Mission & vision

Provide hope and a future for impoverished communities across Honduras

Our mission is to address housing, employment, education, and spiritual needs — together. We call this integral mission: reaching the whole person, not just one dimension of poverty.

CAMINO's vision is a Honduras where families can thrive — with safe homes, quality education, healthcare, and economic opportunity. We're not there yet, but every home we build, every well we dig, and every life we touch moves us closer.

Why Honduras

One of the most impoverished nations in the Western Hemisphere

Honduras faces some of the most severe poverty in Latin America. 73.6% of the population lives in poverty, and 53.7% in extreme poverty — meaning they cannot afford basic food, shelter, or sanitation.

CAMINO focuses on the "super extreme poverty" tier — the 18% with the least access to resources, often living in rural or semi-rural communities with no roads, no clean water, and no economic opportunity.

Our housing-first model reaches families who have been left behind by every other system. Because when nothing else exists, a safe home becomes everything.
