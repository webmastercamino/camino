What we build

Programs that change communities

From a single home to an entire community infrastructure — CAMINO's programs address the full spectrum of what a family needs to thrive.

🏠
Core program

Home Construction

A safe, permanent home is the single biggest change a family in extreme poverty can experience. It ends exposure to rain, parasites, and violence. It gives children a place to study and parents a stable base from which to work.

CAMINO builds each home for approximately $2,000 — a figure that reflects local labor, materials sourced in-country, and the efficiency that comes from doing this at scale. Every home comes with an outreach team that stays connected to the family long after construction ends.

Community infrastructure

Wells & Clean Water

In many of the communities CAMINO serves, women and children walk hours each day to collect water — often from contaminated sources. Clean water changes everything: fewer waterborne illnesses, more time for school and work, and a foundation for community health.

Our well projects provide reliable, clean water access for entire neighborhoods. Each project is accompanied by hygiene education and community stewardship training so infrastructure stays maintained for decades.

💧
🌉
Community infrastructure

Bridges & Roads

Isolated communities miss out on markets, medical care, and schools — not because they're far, but because they're cut off by rivers that flood seasonally. A single bridge can connect a community to the broader world.

CAMINO's bridge and infrastructure projects are designed with local engineers and built by community members, creating local employment while delivering lasting infrastructure.

🌉
🎒
Education

Schools & Playgrounds

Safe, well-equipped schools change a child's relationship to learning. CAMINO builds and renovates school facilities, provides supplies, and creates playgrounds that make education a place children want to be.

We also work directly with teachers and parents to support curriculum improvements and family engagement — because a school building is only the beginning.

🎒
🔨
Economic development

Trade School

Breaking the cycle of generational poverty requires economic opportunity. CAMINO's trade school offers vocational training in construction, electrical work, plumbing, and more — giving young Hondurans marketable skills that open doors to employment and entrepreneurship.

Graduates often go on to work with CAMINO itself — building homes in their own communities and earning a living wage while doing it.

Ministry

Church Planting & Ministry

CAMINO's faith is inseparable from its work. Every community we enter receives not just physical investment but spiritual presence — church planting, pastoral training, and multipurpose ministry centers that serve as the community's gathering place.

These churches become long-term community anchors: hosting after-school programs, support groups, and emergency response when disaster strikes. They're not a project — they're a permanent part of the community.
