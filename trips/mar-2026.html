Preparation

Know before you go

March in Northern Honduras is warm and dry — one of the best times to visit. Check items off as you prepare.

Packing checklist

Passport (valid 6+ months past return date)
Travel insurance documents
Work clothes (4–5 sets you can get dirty)
Closed-toe work boots or sturdy shoes
Work gloves
Hat and sunglasses
Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
Insect repellent (DEET-based)
Casual clothes for evenings
Modest clothing for church services
Prescription medications (extra supply)
Imodium / antidiarrheal medication
Reusable water bottle
$50–100 USD cash for personal purchases
Phone charger (Type A plugs work in Honduras)

Weather in March

Dry season — 78–92°F (26–33°C). Low humidity, sunny, minimal rain. One of the most comfortable months to work outdoors in Honduras.

Money

Honduran lempira (HNL). ATMs in San Pedro Sula. USD accepted at hotels and larger shops. Bring small bills for markets.

Health

Consult your doctor 4–6 weeks before travel. Hepatitis A and Typhoid vaccinations recommended. Drink only bottled or purified water.

Communication

Team house has WiFi. Most U.S. carriers have Honduras plans. CAMINO staff reachable 24/7 via WhatsApp throughout the trip.
