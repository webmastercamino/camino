Preparation

Prep & packing

Check items off as you pack — your progress saves automatically on this device.

✅ Bring this

🌡️ Weather — July in Honduras

Expect 85–95°F (29–35°C) with high humidity. July is rainy season — afternoon and evening showers are common, but mornings are usually clear. The work site is fully exposed. Moisture-wicking fabrics make a real difference. Pack a poncho.

💧 Hydration on site

You will sweat more than you expect. CAMINO provides bottled and purified water throughout the day, but bring your own insulated bottle. Dehydration is the most common health issue — drink before you feel thirsty.

🦟 Mosquitoes & bugs

July rainy season increases mosquito activity. Apply DEET repellent before leaving the team house each morning. Re-apply after lunch. Lightweight long sleeves in the evening are a good idea.

💊 Health & vaccinations

Consult your doctor 4–6 weeks before travel. Recommended: Hepatitis A, Typhoid. Drink only bottled or purified water — CAMINO provides it on site and at the team house. No tap water, no ice from unknown sources.

💱 Money

The lempira (HNL) is local currency. USD is widely accepted in tourist areas. Bring $50–100 USD for markets and personal purchases. ATMs are available in San Pedro Sula. No need for large amounts of cash.

📱 Communication

Most US carriers have Honduras day-pass plans ($5–10/day). WiFi at the team house is reliable. CAMINO staff and Walker are reachable 24/7 via WhatsApp. Tell your family your schedule and that you'll have limited connectivity on build days.

🚫 Leave this at home

Anything that can't survive heat, concrete dust, and one carry-on bag.

💎
Expensive jewelry or watchesLeave it home. The work site is rough and dust gets into everything. CAMINO stores valuables at the team house but theft risk rises with visible wealth.
🧳
More than 1 checked bag + 1 carry-onOne checked bag (50 lbs max) and one carry-on. You won't need more. Overpacking slows the group at SAP and at the team house.
👔
Clothes you care aboutCement, mud, and paint do not come out. Bring clothes you're ready to donate or throw away after the trip. Many participants leave work clothes behind intentionally.
💊
Large quantities of OTC medications to distributeWell-meaning but creates dependency and can cause harm without medical supervision. CAMINO has established protocols for community health needs. Check with Walker first if you want to bring supplies.
🔫
Anything that looks like a weaponIncluding large knives, multi-tools with long blades, or tactical gear. CAMINO operates in community trust environments — this undermines the relationships we've built.
Travel, meals & safety

Logistics

Getting there, getting around, eating well, staying safe, and submitting your documents.

🏥 Health Advisory — Honduras
Honduras endemic health risks for this trip:
  • 🔴 Dengue Fever — endemic, year-round. Use DEET 30%+ repellent at all times outdoors.
  • 🟡 Zika & Chikungunya — present nationwide. Pregnant volunteers: consult OB/GYN before travel.
  • 🟡 Typhoid & Hepatitis A — recommend vaccinations before departure.
  • 🟡 Malaria — low risk in urban San Pedro Sula; higher in rural and coastal areas. Consult travel medicine.
Pre-trip: Visit a travel medicine provider at least 4–6 weeks before departure. Bring travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage.
Emergency: US Embassy Tegucigalpa +504 2236-9320 · Local medevac: AirMed International

✈️ Getting there

Fly into San Pedro Sula (SAP)

San Pedro Sula International is the gateway to Northern Honduras. Look for flights connecting through Miami (MIA), Houston (IAH), or Atlanta (ATL). CAMINO recommends arriving by 4 PM on July 1 to ensure transport to Rio Lindo before dark.

Airport arrival

CAMINO staff holds a sign at baggage claim. Do not exit to the street — wait inside. If your bag is delayed, notify CAMINO staff immediately. They have a protocol. Do not wait alone for luggage.

Return flight — July 7

Depart team house by 5 AM for early flights. Book departures no earlier than 10 AM from SAP to allow for the ~2 hr drive and 2+ hrs of check-in time. CAMINO will confirm your departure pickup time one week before.

🚌 In-country transport

SAP → Rio Lindo

Private CAMINO bus from the airport to the team house in Rio Lindo. Approximately 2 hours through Northern Honduras. No individual cars needed — everyone travels together.

Daily site transport

CAMINO vehicles pick up the group from the team house each morning and return each afternoon. Build site is approximately 15 minutes from the team house. No navigation needed.

No rental cars needed

All group transportation is included in your trip. CAMINO strongly discourages individual vehicles — it creates security and coordination issues. Stay with the group for all movements.

🍽️ Meals

All meals included

Breakfast, lunch, and dinner are included every day. Meals at the team house and on site are provided by CAMINO and local families.

Local Honduran food

Expect rice, beans, plantains, fresh fruit, eggs, grilled meats, and tortillas. Food is simple, filling, and delicious. Most people love it.

Dietary restrictions

Accommodated with advance notice. Email Walker at least 2 weeks before departure with any allergies or dietary needs. Vegetarian is straightforward. Vegan and gluten-free require planning.

Safe drinking water

Bottled and purified water is provided throughout the trip — at the team house and on the build site. Never drink tap water. No ice from unknown sources.

Snacks

Morning and afternoon snacks provided on the build site. Bring a few personal snacks if you have specific needs. Local chips, crackers, and fruit are available at small stores near the team house.

Meals out

Do not eat at local restaurants without CAMINO staff present. Food safety protocols exist for a reason — your stomach is not adapted to local water exposure the way locals are.

Emergency protocols

1
Medical issue: Notify CAMINO staff immediately. Minor injuries: team house has a first-aid kit and a staff member trained in first aid. For anything serious, CAMINO will transport you to Hospital Leonardo Martínez in San Pedro Sula (~40 min). Do not attempt to self-transport.
2
Lost passport: Contact CAMINO staff, then call the U.S. Embassy (+504 2236-9320). Your flight booking and a photo ID copy (carry one separately from your passport) will help expedite an emergency travel document. Do not panic — CAMINO has navigated this before.
3
Separated from the group: Stay in a public, well-lit area. Call Walker immediately. Do not wander. Do not attempt to navigate independently. Stay put and wait for CAMINO pickup. This is a very low likelihood scenario — CAMINO counts heads constantly.
4
Security concern: Alert CAMINO staff immediately — do not engage or confront. CAMINO field staff have deep local relationships and will adjust plans immediately. Trust their judgment about when and how to move.
5
Flight cancellation / disruption: Contact CAMINO before rebooking anything. We have a protocol for extended stays and can arrange accommodations. Do not book hotels or alternate flights without checking with Walker first.
6
Mental health / emotional distress: This week can be emotionally intense. If you're struggling, tell your trip coordinator or Walker. CAMINO staff are experienced with this. There is no shame in asking for support.
