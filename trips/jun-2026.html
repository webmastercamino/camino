Preparation

Know before you go

June is rainy season in Honduras — plan for heat and afternoon showers. Pack accordingly.

Packing checklist

Passport (valid 6+ months past return)
Travel insurance documents
Work clothes — pack more for a 10-day trip (6–7 sets)
Rain poncho or light rain jacket (rainy season)
Closed-toe work boots — waterproof is a bonus
Work gloves
Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
Strong insect repellent (DEET 30%+) — rainy season means more mosquitoes
Quick-dry towel
Prescription medications (bring extra for a 10-day trip)
Large reusable water bottle — stay hydrated in summer heat
$100–150 USD cash for personal purchases and optional excursions

Weather in June

Rainy season begins. Expect 82–94°F (28–34°C) with afternoon thundershowers most days. Mornings are clear — build work happens early. Pack a rain layer.

Two homes in 10 days

The longer trip means teams can often complete two full home builds. CAMINO staff will brief you on the second family during the trip.

Health

Rainy season increases mosquito activity. DEET repellent is important. Consult your doctor about malaria risk — prophylaxis may be recommended for longer stays in some areas.

Copán ruins

The mid-trip Saturday free day includes the option to visit the Copán Mayan ruins — a UNESCO World Heritage Site about 2 hours from our build area. Highly recommended.
