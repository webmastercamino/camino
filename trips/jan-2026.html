💊 Health & Medical

  • No required vaccinations, but Hepatitis A & Typhoid are strongly recommended. See your doctor 4–6 weeks before departure.
  • Drink bottled water only. Ice in restaurants is usually safe but when in doubt, skip it.
  • Bring anti-malarial medication if your doctor recommends it for your trip zone.
  • CAMINO has a first aid kit on site and a protocol for serious medical emergencies (see Emergency tab).
  • If you have a chronic condition, share it with your trip leader before you go.

💵 Money & Payments

  • US Dollars are accepted in most places. Local currency is the Honduran Lempira (HNL) — ~25 HNL per $1 USD.
  • Bring $200–$300 cash for souvenirs, personal purchases, and tips.
  • ATMs exist in San Pedro Sula but notify your bank before traveling.
  • Credit cards are accepted at larger stores and restaurants.
  • Do not flash large amounts of cash in public.

📱 Phone & Connectivity

  • Check if your carrier offers an international day pass — often $10/day and worth it.
  • Wi-Fi is available at accommodations. Download offline maps of San Pedro Sula and Rio Lindo before you fly.
  • WhatsApp is the primary way Hondurans communicate — download it before departure.
  • Keep your phone secure. Use a secure pouch inside your bag on the street.
  • CAMINO staff will have local SIM cards for group communication.

🌡️ Weather & Climate

  • January is dry season in Honduras — warm days (82–88°F / 28–31°C), cooler evenings (65–70°F).
  • Bring sunscreen and a hat. The UV index is very high, especially at altitude.
  • Mornings can be breezy. A light jacket for early departure is useful.
  • Rain is rare in January but pack a lightweight rain layer just in case.

🤝 Cultural Tips

  • Hondurans are warm and hospitable — accept food and hospitality graciously, even if you're full.
  • Greet everyone with a handshake or cheek kiss. Eye contact matters.
  • Dress modestly, especially for church visits.
  • Ask before photographing people — always.
  • Learning 10–15 Spanish phrases will transform your trip. "¿Cómo te llamas?" (What's your name?) goes a long way.
  • Be present. Put your phone away during meals and conversations with families.

✈️ Travel Tips

  • Fly into Tegucigalpa (SAP) — Aeropuerto Internacional Ramón Villeda Morales (SAP). Not SAP (San Pedro Sula).
  • Arrive on time — CAMINO staff wait at arrivals but have a limited window.
  • Keep one carry-on with essentials in case checked bags are delayed.
  • The airport is small and fast — you'll clear customs in 20–30 minutes usually.
  • Declare any items over $500 in value at customs if asked.

🏥 Medical Emergency Protocol

1
Stay calm and assess Is the person conscious and breathing? Do not move someone if a spinal injury is possible.
2
Call CAMINO emergency line Call +1 (XXX) XXX-XXXX immediately. They will direct next steps and coordinate local response.
3
Call 911 if life-threatening If the person is not breathing or you cannot reach CAMINO, call 911 and request an ambulance. Send one team member to flag the vehicle.
4
Do not leave the person alone Assign two people to stay with them at all times. A third person handles communication.
5
Notify the U.S. Embassy For serious events (hospitalization, death, arrest), the embassy must be notified. CAMINO staff will assist with this.
6
Call travel insurance File a report with your travel insurer within 24 hours of any medical event requiring professional treatment.
