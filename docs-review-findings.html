Impact Stories — Real, Usable, Not Currently on Site

Hurricane Eta & Iota Resilience (November 2020)

Two Category 4 hurricanes struck Honduras within two weeks of each other in November 2020. CAMINO-built homes survived without losing roofs while surrounding structures were destroyed. This is a powerful proof-of-quality story and donor retention narrative — "your gift builds to last."

Juan Carlos — Community Member Turned Apprentice

Juan Carlos was a community member and CAMINO scholarship recipient who became Walker's house-building apprentice. This is the "sustainable employment" impact story the site currently describes abstractly — Juan Carlos makes it real.

Kelsey's Missions (Culpeper, VA) — House #33

Kelsey's Missions from Culpeper funded the 33rd house specifically. The Culpeper July 2026 trip portal already references this church — this connection predates that trip and adds historical context.

Belen Water Well — $6,500 Real Cost

The Belen community water well came in at ~$6,500 (under the $25K estimate because water was found at a shallower depth). Good "donor dollars go far" story and a real infrastructure milestone to add to the impact timeline.

Guatemala Vision

By 2020 Walker had made connections in Guatemala and begun K'iche language study. The long-term vision is "all of Central America, not just Honduras." This supports the CNN Heroes / 15-year anniversary narrative on the roadmap.
