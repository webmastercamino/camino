Executive summary
Where CAMINO stands — and where the opportunity is
This is a strong foundation. The redesign delivers something most small nonprofits never build — a credible, visually coherent, multi-pathway site with real content. But the gap between "good site" and "growth engine" is still significant. Five critical conversion blockers need to be resolved first, then a content and trust layer will unlock the major donor and corporate giving channels that are currently closed.
✅

Conversion-ready design

The visual hierarchy, CTAs, and brand story are strong enough to convert warm traffic. Trip pages are best-in-class for a small nonprofit. The $2,000 home stat is a killer value proposition that maps directly to a giving decision.

⚠️

Broken at the seams

The contact form doesn't work. The store is invisible to most visitors. There's no financial transparency or prayer page — both essential for faith-based donors. These are fixing problems, not adding features, and they should be Week 1 priorities.

🚀

Untapped upside

Corporate church partnerships, peer-to-peer fundraising, and a blog/field-update stream could each 2–3× annual donor revenue. These require 2–6 weeks each but have a compounding effect — they permanently expand the top of the funnel.

What's working
Competitive advantages to protect and build on

🏠 The $2,000 stat is a killer ask

A specific, believable, emotionally resonant number tied directly to a complete deliverable. World Vision charges $39/month (opaque). CAMINO says "$2,000 = one family's life changes forever." That maps to a gift decision. Don't dilute it.

✈️ Trip portal is best-in-class

Countdown timer, day-by-day schedule, persistent packing checklist, emergency protocols, WhatsApp group link. No competitor site at this budget level comes close. This is a genuine differentiator that builds trust and reduces pre-trip anxiety.

📣 Testimonials have emotional specificity

"When Walker handed her the key, she just held it and shook." That's a real moment. Most nonprofits run generic quote filler. CAMINO's testimonials are the kind that get forwarded in a church email. Expand the archive.

⛪ Faith-authentic without gatekeeping

The site clearly states the Christian mission but never makes non-believers feel excluded. This is rare and extends the audience. Maintain it — don't water it down, but don't add language that reads as inside-group signaling.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Founder story is personal and credible

Walker and Seiny Somerville's story is visible and specific. Founded 2014. First trip was a turning point. 115+ homes. This is the kind of origin story that drives major donor relationships. Most nonprofits bury the founder — CAMINO leads with them.

📐 Multi-pathway engagement model

The site already serves three distinct audiences (donors, volunteers, partners) with tailored messaging — without making anyone feel wrong-addressed. That architecture is more sophisticated than many organizations 10× CAMINO's size.

Priority gaps
Top 15 missing pages & features
Ranked by impact × urgency. High-priority items are conversion blockers or trust failures. Medium items expand reach and revenue. Low items are SEO/polish that compound over time.
#1 · High Priority
Fix the contact form
The form action still reads YOUR_FORM_ID. Every inquiry from a potential volunteer or donor silently fails. This is the most important 15-minute fix on the entire site.
Critical Contact Effort
#2 · High Priority
Financial transparency page
The site claims "100% to Honduras" and "transparent & accountable" but provides zero evidence. Link to the 990-N on ProPublica, publish a simple budget breakdown, or add a one-page annual report. Major donors won't give without this.
Trust Blocker Donor Effort
#3 · High Priority
Prayer page / prayer request
Every peer mission org (Praying Pelican, Adventures in Missions, World Vision) has a dedicated prayer section. Faith donors who can't give money often give prayer — and those people become your future donors and ambassadors. A prayer page costs almost nothing to build.
Faith Essential Engagement Effort
#4 · High Priority
Privacy policy & terms
Footer links to "#" (dead). Collecting email addresses, contact form data, and trip inquiries without a published privacy policy creates legal exposure. A minimal one-page policy fixes this and is required by Gmail, Mailchimp, and most email providers.
Legal Risk Compliance Effort
#5 · High Priority
Swag store in main nav
The store exists and all 4 trip pages are built, but it's invisible from the primary navigation on most pages. Volunteers are the store's core customer — and they access the site via the homepage. The store is currently hidden from them.
Revenue Navigation Effort
#6 · Medium Priority
Video content — even one video
A 90-second video of a home dedication ceremony would outperform every block of text on the site. Faith nonprofits with video see 3–4× higher email signup and giving rates. Even a phone-recorded walkthrough from Walker would serve the purpose.
Engagement Content Effort
#7 · Medium Priority
Field blog / trip updates
A simple blog with 1–2 posts per trip keeps donors engaged between giving cycles and provides 20–40 SEO-indexed pages per year. Competitors publishing weekly field updates have 3–10× more organic traffic. Even a single post per trip adds up fast.
SEO + Retention Content Effort
#8 · Medium Priority
Corporate & church partnership track
The "Adopt a community" card on give.html mentions corporate giving but doesn't have its own page, pricing, or contact flow. Church partnerships ($5,000–$50,000+/year) are the highest-leverage funding track for CAMINO's model. This needs a dedicated page.
Revenue Corporate Effort
#9 · Medium Priority
Impact dashboard / home map
115+ homes built since 2014 with no visual proof. A simple numbered grid or an interactive Google Map showing home locations gives donors a visceral sense of scale. "Home #47 — Rodríguez family — 2022" is more convincing than any stat bar.
Trust + Conversion Impact Effort
#10 · Medium Priority
Volunteer opportunities beyond trips
The site assumes all volunteers travel to Honduras. Most won't. Grant writers, social media coordinators, church liaisons, and fundraising volunteers are equally valuable — and there are far more of them. A US-based volunteer page opens a new segment of engaged supporters.
Engagement Volunteer Effort
#11 · Medium Priority
Partner & church network page
Social proof from partner churches, funding organizations, or NGOs dramatically increases donor trust. A "Church partners" section with logos — even 5–10 churches — changes the perception from "small startup" to "established mission network." Ask current church partners for logo permission.
Trust Credibility Effort
#12 · Lower Priority
501(c)(3) verification link
Footer claims "501(c)(3) Non-Profit" but provides no verification link. Add a link to the CAMINO listing on ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer or IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search. First-time donors often check before giving.
Trust Compliance Effort
#13 · Lower Priority
Schema.org structured data
Adding JSON-LD for NonProfit Organization and Event types helps Google display rich results — organization name, description, social links, and trip events in search. This is a set-once SEO improvement with lasting benefit.
SEO Technical Effort
#14 · Lower Priority
Spanish language option
A Spanish version of at least the homepage and programs page opens the site to Spanish-speaking donors in the US (large demographic), Honduran diaspora, and community partners. Could start as a single translated landing page before expanding.
Reach Localization Effort
#15 · Lower Priority
Story archive & impact library
Stories page has 3 stories. CAMINO has built 115+ homes. Each home is a story, and each story is SEO content, donor evidence, and re-sharing fuel. A browsable archive — even just 12–20 stories — transforms the site from a brochure into a living record of impact.
Impact Content Effort
Implementation plan
3-phase roadmap
Ordered so that each phase removes a blocker that would limit the ROI of the next phase. Fix what's broken → build what's missing → scale what's working.
1
Fix It
Weeks 1–2
🔧
Activate Formspree contact form

Create a free account at formspree.io, replace YOUR_FORM_ID in contact.html, test end-to-end. 15 minutes. Unblocks primary lead capture.

🔒
Publish privacy policy & terms

Create privacy.html with minimal language covering email collection, contact forms, and cookies. Fix the dead "#" links in every footer.

🗺️
Add swag store to main nav

Currently hidden from all main pages. Add "Store" link alongside Contact in the desktop nav — one PowerShell pass across all pages.

📊
Wire up Mailchimp newsletter forms

Replace the placeholder Mailchimp form action on index.html and stories.html with real u= and id= params. Newsletter captures are currently not being saved.

🔍
Submit sitemap to Google Search Console

sitemap.xml is now live. Register Google Search Console for the GitHub Pages domain, submit the sitemap, verify ownership. Unlocks performance tracking.

2
Build It
Weeks 3–8
🙏
Create prayer page

A prayer.html page with current CAMINO prayer needs (active trips, families, Walker & Seiny, Honduras context), a prayer request form, and a "prayer partner" email list opt-in. Faith donors who can't give money give prayer first.

📋
Publish transparency / annual report

Create a one-page transparency.html with budget breakdown, 990-N filing link (ProPublica), homes built per year, and a simple impact statement. This is the #1 objection-handler for major donors.

🏢
Build corporate & church partnership page

A dedicated partnerships.html with 3–4 tiers (Single Home Sponsor, Multi-Home Partner, Community Adoption), use cases for churches vs. businesses, an application form, and logos of current partners.

🤝
Add US-based volunteer opportunities

Expand about.html or create volunteer.html to describe US-based roles: grant writing, social media, church liaisons, fundraising events. Add an application form. Converts interested visitors who can't travel.

📹
Record & embed a 90-second video

Walker's phone camera is enough. One video of a home dedication ceremony, embedded in index.html above the fold. No editing required — raw emotion converts. YouTube embed is free and boosts SEO.

✍️
Blog / field updates — Decap CMS

Add a free, open-source headless CMS (Decap, formerly Netlify CMS) that writes directly to the GitHub repo. Staff get a browser-based "New post" editor with no code required. Each post auto-publishes to the site via a GitHub commit and generates an RSS feed for downstream social automation.

📲
Auto-post blog entries to Facebook & Instagram

Wire the blog RSS feed to IFTTT or Make (free tiers). When a post publishes, it auto-fires to the Facebook Page and queues in Buffer for Instagram. No backend code needed — connect three services in ~30 minutes. One "Publish" click in the CMS = website + all social platforms simultaneously.

🏭
Corporate mission trip page

A dedicated page targeting HR/CSR departments at faith-friendly companies. Pitch: CAMINO handles all logistics — their team shows up at the airport. Frame as a more impactful alternative to local Habitat for Humanity days: deeper cross-cultural experience, complete trip support, tangible deliverable (one home built). Include group size tiers (6, 12, 25 people), team pricing, and a "request a proposal" inquiry form.

3
Scale It
Months 3–6
📰
Launch field blog

One post per trip, 400–800 words, written by a volunteer. Each post is SEO content that indexes for "Honduras mission trip 2026," "CAMINO Honduras," and related searches. 8–10 posts/year = 8–10 new organic entry points per year, compounding.

🗺️
Impact map / home archive

Build a visual grid or map of all 115+ homes — home number, family name (with permission), year, and a 2-sentence story. This is the most powerful proof asset CAMINO has. Donors see their home on the map. They share it.

📱
Spanish language landing page

A single translated index page targeting Spanish-speaking donors and the Honduran diaspora in the US. Even a basic /es/ page with the mission statement, give link, and contact form opens a new audience segment.

🎁
Interactive gift catalog

Replace the static cost breakdown with an interactive catalog: $50 = cement bags (select quantity), $500 = a week of local labor, $2,000 = one home (name the family). Add a "Give this as a gift" flow for Christmas/birthday giving.

📈
Analytics & conversion tracking

Add Google Analytics 4 + goals (form submission, Give Now click, trip reservation, newsletter signup). With data, every subsequent decision is defensible. Without it, CAMINO is flying blind on what converts.

Strategic leverage
How to use this design to accelerate CAMINO's mission
The redesign isn't just an upgrade — it's a platform. These are the high-leverage plays specific to CAMINO's model that the current design enables.
✈️

Trip portal as the primary lead magnet

The trip portal pages (with countdown, schedule, and checklist) are unusual enough to drive word-of-mouth. When Trip Coordinator A shares it with Trip Coordinator B at a different church, CAMINO's quality is immediately visible. That's recruitment.

Multiplier: volunteer acquisition
🏠

The key ceremony as a recurring content event

Every home completion is a shareable moment — video, photo, quote. If Walker sends a 30-second video after each dedication, it becomes a story on the website, an email blast, an Instagram post, and a church slide. One event, five touchpoints.

Multiplier: donor engagement
📣

Trip alumni as a distribution channel

Every trip participant returns as a potential ambassador. The stories page asks them to share — but there's no formal alumni track. A simple "CAMINO alumni" email segment with a monthly field update becomes a 100-person+ outreach network at no cost.

Multiplier: organic reach
⛪

Church-as-partner model

The highest-leverage nonprofit model is church adoption — one congregation commits to CAMINO as their global missions partner, sends annual trips, and funds 3–5 homes/year. The partnerships page enables a formal pitch. At 5 church partners, CAMINO triples capacity without a single new donor.

Multiplier: capacity & funding
💛

Swag as missions awareness

A CAMINO hat worn by a past volunteer to church, the gym, or the office is a passive billboard. When someone asks "what's CAMINO Honduras?" the answer opens a door. The swag store isn't just revenue — it's distributed awareness among exactly the demographic most likely to give.

Multiplier: brand visibility
🙏

Prayer as the lowest-barrier entry point

Not everyone can give $2,000. Not everyone can travel to Honduras. But almost anyone can commit to praying for a family once a week. Prayer partners become email subscribers. Email subscribers become donors. This is the most underused funnel in faith nonprofits.

Multiplier: donor pipeline
Tools & integrations
Recommended nonprofit tech stack
All recommended tools have free tiers appropriate for CAMINO's current scale. Prioritize the "needed" tools first — they unblock the highest-priority gaps.
📋

Formspree

Needed now

Fix the contact form in 15 minutes. Free tier handles 50 submissions/month. Upgrade to $10/month if volume grows. Required for Phase 1.

📧

Mailchimp

Needed now

Newsletter list is built — the u= and id= params need to be filled in. Free up to 500 contacts. The single most important donor retention tool.

💰

Give Lively

Needed — Phase 2

Free for nonprofits. Replaces the placeholder "caminohonduras.com" give links with a real donation page. Supports one-time, monthly, and peer-to-peer fundraising.

👕

Printful

Needed — Phase 2

Fulfills the swag store orders automatically. CAMINO sets retail price, Printful bills base cost, CAMINO keeps the markup. Zero inventory, zero risk.

📊

Google Analytics 4

Needed — Phase 3

Free. Tracks every visitor, page, CTA click, and conversion. Without it, you can't know which pages drive donations or which trip listing converts the most reservations.

🔍

Google Search Console

Needed — Phase 1

Free. Submit the new sitemap.xml to index all 17 pages. Monitor which search queries bring visitors. Takes 20 minutes to set up, shows results in 2–4 weeks.

✍️

Decap CMS

Needed — Phase 2

Free, open-source headless CMS (formerly Netlify CMS). Adds a browser-based blog editor that writes posts directly to the GitHub repo — no code required. One "Publish" click deploys to the live site and generates an RSS feed for social automation.

🔁

IFTTT / Make

Needed — Phase 2

Free tier automation. Watch the blog RSS feed — when a post publishes, auto-fire a Facebook Page post and queue an Instagram post via Buffer. Three connected services, ~30 minutes to set up. Makes every blog post a multi-channel distribution event.

📱

Buffer

Needed — Phase 2

Free tier: 3 social channels, 10 queued posts. The delivery end of the IFTTT automation — receives auto-queued posts from blog RSS and publishes to Facebook and Instagram on a schedule. Replaces manual copy-paste posting.

🎥

YouTube

Optional — Phase 2

Free hosting for trip videos. Even phone footage of a home dedication embedded on the homepage would outperform every other content asset on the site within 30 days.

Stretch goal
CNN Heroes readiness — can CAMINO sustain the exposure?
CNN Heroes is CNN's annual series honoring everyday people changing the world. Past honorees have seen their organizations receive $50K–$250K+ in donations in a single week. The real question isn't whether CAMINO deserves it — it does. The question is whether the org can absorb and convert a sudden, massive surge in interest without dropping the ball.
📋

What CNN Heroes requires

✗ Compelling visual story — CNN produces a 3-minute segment. Walker and a home dedication are perfect, but they need professional or near-professional footage.
✗ Financial transparency verified — CNN vets nominees thoroughly. Charity Navigator rating or GuideStar Gold Seal is effectively required.
✗ Measurable impact at scale — 150+ homes with documented outcomes (family names, before/after, years) makes the number credible and emotional.
✓ Clear, simple mission — "$2,000 builds a home for a family" is as clean as it gets. This is already CNN Heroes-caliber messaging.
✓ Founder-driven story — Walker and Seiny's origin story (first trip, turning point, 12 years later) is exactly the kind of personal narrative CNN Heroes is built on.
⚡

Surge readiness checklist

If CAMINO airs on CNN, 5,000–50,000 people could visit the site in 48 hours. This is what needs to be in place before that happens:

✗ Donation platform that won't crash under load (Give Lively handles this — it's built for surges)
✗ Email auto-responder for new donors — "Thank you, here's what your gift builds"
✗ A volunteer to monitor and respond to the contact form 24/7 for the first week
✗ Trip capacity to handle 10× normal interest — a waitlist system and rapid-response booking flow
✗ GitHub Pages serves static files and won't go down under traffic — no action needed on hosting
✗ A "surge landing page" — a single URL Walker can give on-air that goes straight to Give Lively
🎯
Verdict

Yes — but not yet. Target 2028 nomination for a 2029 airing.

CAMINO's story is CNN Heroes-caliber today. What isn't in place yet: the financial transparency documentation, professional video, Give Lively infrastructure, and surge-response capacity. Two years of Phase 2–3 work closes those gaps. A 2028 nomination — timed to CAMINO's 14th year, building toward the 15th anniversary campaign — sets up a perfect storyline: "The org turning 15 that's built 150+ homes with nothing but faith and volunteers." That's a CNN segment that writes itself. Make 2027 the year to pursue a Charity Navigator rating and shoot the first professional video.

🏠

The bottom line

CAMINO Honduras already has a story worth sharing, a model worth funding, and a team worth trusting. The website's job is to make that visible and make the path from "interested" to "involved" as short as possible. Most of the gaps on this list are a few hours of work each. The upside is a site that works as hard as Walker does.
